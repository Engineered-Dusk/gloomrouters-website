What it is

A tactical fight over temporary light

Command Mechs, build routing infrastructure, and contest Relics in a hostile playfield split between bright Corridors and the Gloom. Every round starts with route selection, then turns into a pressure puzzle of deployments, hidden threats, and engagement timing.

Routes

Choose the light package that powers your round.

Nodes

Anchor the field with prisms, lenses, and relays.

Modules

Upgrade Mechs and bend combat math before impact.

Directives

Fire off tactical orders at the moment they hurt most.

Game Overview

How Gloom Routers plays

Two players build a playfield, choose Routes, and win engagements to claim Relics before the site goes dark.

1

Start with a crew

Each player brings a deck and a Route package.

2

Route pathing

Each player chooses and reveals a route card, adding strategy and flavor to the current round.

3

Turn Order

Turn order choice is awarded to one player. They may then choose who goes first for both Deployment and Engagement.

4

Deployment

Spend Cores to place Mechs, Nodes, Modules, and Directives onto the playfield.

5

Engagement

Each player may choose to engage with the other using their deployed Mechs. Hidden Gloom Mechs and Directive cards ensure the engagements keep both players on their toes.

Choose up to 2 of the following engagement choices:

  • Combat
  • Relic
  • Tactical
6

Aftermath

After a defense declaration, a winner is declared, then abilities and modifiers are resolved.

7

Armistice

A brief pause in hostile engagement in which card effects may trigger.

8

Reset

Committed Mechs are readied, abilities are triggered, and the board is set to a refresh state before returning to the Route Pathing round.

Winning the game

The first player to reach 10 Relics wins. If the shared, public Relic pool (Relic Site) reaches zero, players enter a sudden death round.
