Gloom Routers

Route the light. Survive the dark.

Gloom Routers is a dark sci-fi tactical card/board game about rival expedition machines fighting over engineered light corridors in a ruined world.

Play vs Bot

What it is

Command Mechs, contest Relics, and build a strong playfield. Gloom Routers focuses on tactical pathing, high-pressure engagements, and board-state control through cards.

Game Overview

How Gloom Routers Plays

Gloom Routers is a two-player card battle about building a playfield, choosing Routes, and winning engagements to claim Relics.

1

Starting Out

Each player starts with a deck, a Route package, a hand of cards, Cores, and Relics. Cores are the resource you spend to play cards. Relics are how you pull ahead and win.

2

Route Pathing

Each round begins with Route Pathing. Both players choose a Route, then reveal them together. Routes give you Cores, cards, and Magnitude for the round. A Pathing card is also revealed, changing the round in some way.

3

Turn Order

Next, one player chooses turn order. This usually gives the player who is behind a little control over how the round unfolds.

4

Deployment

During Deployment, players spend Cores to add Mechs, Nodes, Modules, and Directives to the playfield.

5

Engagements

Most fights are decided by Engagement Value (EV). EV is the strength of your Mechs, plus bonuses from cards already on the playfield.

During Engagements, players attack with Ready Mechs and choose one of three engagement types:

  • Combat pressures the opponent’s playfield.
  • Relic engagements take Relics.
  • Tactical engagements create utility swings, like readying Mechs, committing enemy Mechs, drawing cards, or disrupting the opponent.

The defender may block with Ready Mechs, or let the attack go uncontested. Then compare attacking EV to defending EV. Higher EV wins; ties go to the attacker.

6

After The Fight

Aftermath effects happen after an engagement resolves.

7

End Of Round

After both players finish engaging, Armistice and Refresh clean up the round, ready cards, pay out refresh effects, and start the next round.

8

Winning

If the Relic Site runs out and the game is still tied, sudden death rounds continue until one player pulls ahead.
